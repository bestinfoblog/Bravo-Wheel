The Burden of Big Decisions  

 

Major life decisions churn up stress and overthinking. Without a clear path, your mind races, considering hypotheticals and outcomes that may never come to fruition. You play out an endless mental tug-of-war, trying to rationalize what is best for your future.

 

For example, when debating a career pivot, you agonize over the risks of leaving stability behind, the skills you would need to gain, the pay cut you may have to take, and how drastically your lifestyle could change. These imagined projections paralyze you from making a move in any direction.

 

With relationships, your head spins between the comfort of the known and the idealized promise of someone new. Or you may deliberate having kids because you can envision both the rewards and freedom of remaining child-free. Every potential choice seems to have endless pros and cons.

Another benefit of injecting randomness into decision-making is activating the beginner’s mind. A beginner's mind refers to an attitude of openness, curiosity, and presence that allows you to see things anew. It is the antidote to rigid assumptions and old thought patterns that can lead us astray.

 

When you spin the wheel, you have to orient yourself to give the prompt or advice presented an unbiased hearing. Like a beginner, you ponder it without preconceived notions. This allows you to see angles to your dilemma that your worn thought grooves would normally filter out.

 

Putting decision power in the hands of the wheel also lets go of the need to control outcomes. You release the tight grip on rationalizing every angle in hopes of picking perfectly. Leaning into the wisdom of chance opens you up to new possibilities. There is power when you surrender to receiving a nudge down a path you may not have consciously chosen.

 

Progress Over Perfection
